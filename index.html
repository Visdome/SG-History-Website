About This Project

Singapore is one of the fastest-connected countries in the world today. Gigabit fibre connections are commonplace, and low latency is expected as standard. But it wasn't always like this.

This website documents the transformation of Singapore's internet infrastructure over the past 26 years — from dial-up modems and shared phone lines to nationwide 5G and 10Gbps fibre.

This website examines how improvements in internet infrastructure transformed online gaming experiences in Singapore between 2000 and 2026. By looking at how gaming changed over time, we can trace the evolution of the internet itself.

Three Eras of Connectivity

Singapore's internet history can be divided into three distinct periods, each with its own character and impact on how people used the internet.

2000 – 2009

Shared and Slow

Dial-up connections, early ADSL broadband, and the rise of cybercafés as the main place to game online.

Typical ping: 150–300ms
2010 – 2019

The Fibre Revolution

OpenNet's national fibre rollout transformed home internet. Gigabit speeds became normal.

Typical ping: 8–25ms
2020 – 2026

Ultra-Low Latency

5G and 10Gbps fibre made lag almost unnoticeable. Mobile gaming became mainstream.

Typical ping: <5ms
